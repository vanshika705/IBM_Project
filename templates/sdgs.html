Overview of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a collection of 17 global goals set by the United Nations General Assembly in 2015. They are designed to address the most pressing issues facing our world, including poverty, inequality, climate change, environmental degradation, peace, and justice.

In the context of health and education, the SDGs aim to ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages, as well as ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all.

Our Focus on SDGs

Goal 3: Good Health and Well-being

We are dedicated to improving health outcomes by enhancing access to healthcare services, promoting preventive care, and addressing health disparities.

Goal 4: Quality Education

Our initiatives focus on providing quality education and lifelong learning opportunities, supporting educational institutions, and promoting inclusive education.
